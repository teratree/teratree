Strategy

Mission, Vision, Objectives, Strategy, Tactics Exercise

Background notes: If you leave it, it will become beautiful

Mission

  • Have fun (people)
  • Help wildlife (land animals)
  • Reverse climate change (sea animals)

Vision

Landowners, growers and planters, empowered to make the bold decision to shape their own environment for the benefit of the themselves, and the wildlife around them, and to reverse climate change which will also give the world's reefs a chance to return.

We're not so interested in dwelling on:

  • species loss
  • food production
  • natural disasters e.g. flooding
  • war
These are all negative things, and we want our brand to be immensily positive. Strategy * Identify land that could be used for planting (ideally in corridors) * Identify the best trees to be planted on that land (based on soil and the types of animals that could thrive in that environment) * Commission the growing of seedlings of the required varieties * Encourage everyone to plant one tree per year for their lifetimes Operations * Design hypotheses * Quickly run build-measure-learn experiments to test the hypotheses * Delegate as much of the hypothesis and experiment work as possible to participants Tactics * Minimum, minimum, minimum (includes efficiency as a value - don't want to produce more carbon in website costs than is saved) * Use BGS soil data * Use OS building data combined with satellite imagary to identify areas * See the barriers that prevent people actually planting trees and tackle them * Keep the planter experience incredibly simple, look at park run as an example The Why not?
